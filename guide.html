Rise in Extra Time Guide

Getting Started

1. Start by securing a small island and focus on resource production

2. Build up your forces gradually with a mix of units

3. Scout nearby islands to plan your expansion

Unit Strengths & Weaknesses

Naki Units

- Guard Naki: Strong defender, weak against Forest Spirit

- Druid Naki: Good support unit, vulnerable to Nyxi

- Spotter Naki: Excellent scout unit

Spirit Units

- Forest Spirit: Strong against Guard Naki

- Grass Spirit: Effective against Nyxi

- Elder Spirit: Powerful but vulnerable to Druid Naki

Advanced Strategies

1. Use artifacts strategically for maximum benefit

2. Choose offense and defense rarely, go either full attack, full defense, or a mix of attack + speed/defense + speed / pure speed (only good for PvE, rarely useful in PvP except when against an attacker)

3. Form alliances with nearby players when beneficial

4. Always keep some units in reserve for defense

Game Terminology

Empire & Properties

- Regions: Distinct areas with unique characteristics and strategic value. Contains islands.

- Island: Distinct land where you can recruit certain types of troops and mine soulstone. Contains fields.

- Fields: Smallest land mass you can claim. Each field can have 3 slots. Each slot can either mine soulstone or recruit certain troop. One slot can be active at a time

Combat & Strategy

- Army: Collection of military units for defense and offense

- Conquer: Taking control of opponent territories

Progression & Rewards

- Leaderboard: Player ranking system based on competitive performance

- Treasures: Discoverable items providing various benefits

- Ancient Artifacts: Special items that unlock empire powers and abilities

Multiplayer Elements

- Team: Group of collaborating players working toward common goals

- Explore: Discovering new areas, resources, and strategic positions

Advanced Combat Tactics

Quick Stab (A.K.A. counter)

- When your field is under attack instead of defending it normally, you send attack troops which arrive after your opponent's attack land.

- The delay between the attacks is often milliseconds.

- The defense later converts into attack when your opponent claims the field.

Spam

- Continuously keep attacking the same island with small amount of troops

- Other people won't be able to see who is attacking and might send a stronger attack, weakening your opponent's defence.

Bait

- Putting troops on a field and then removing them in a small time (often milliseconds) before attack lands and plaguing enemy (enemy got baited).

- Enemies use Spotter Naki to look at what units you have.

- Plague kill 90% of units (even if there is no defender on field)
